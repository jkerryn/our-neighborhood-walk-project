# Our-Neighborhood-Walk-Project

This project aims to explore the role of cartographic design and personal data visualization in advancing understanding of the dynamic, co-constructed sense-of-place that occurs between human and canine while walking together. Sense-of-place is a geographic concept used to explore how individuals perceive, experience, and develop emotional connections to particular locations. Individuals’ perceptions of the natural and built environment are further shaped by their interactions with companion dogs in those environments. Dogs, too, develop their own sense-of-place based on their personal interests in combination with the route(s) prescribed by their guardians. Sense-of-place and dog walking have important implications for the health and well-being of both pet guardians and canines. Dog walking is an important strategy for promoting physical activity, reducing chronic disease, and increasing environmental awareness. Deeper connections with the environment foster a stronger and more positive sense-of-place, which translates to greater emotional, psychological, and social well-being and promotes community-building.

In this project, we explore three interrelated research questions:
  R1) How do objective and perceived elements of the built and natural environment influence the co-constructed experience of walking together?
  R2) Can geo-tagged biometric data – collected for both pet guardians and canines – help to contextualize the sense-of-place experienced while walking together?
  R3) How can interactive storymap depictions of objective/perceived elements of place combined with personal biometric data be designed to:
    (a) facilitate greater awareness of human-pet-environment interactions, 
    (b) enable reflection on/reminiscence about past walks with dogs,
    (c) enhance future interactions with pets and places, and
    (d) increase pet guardians’ trust of their personal data?
